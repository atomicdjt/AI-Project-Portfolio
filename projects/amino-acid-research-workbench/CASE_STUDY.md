# Amino Acid Research Workbench — Case Study

## Summary

The Amino Acid Research Workbench is an explainable educational bioinformatics tool for comparing amino acid substitutions and generating structured evidence dossiers.

## Problem

Students and early learners often struggle to connect mutation notation to biochemical meaning. They may know that an amino acid changed but not understand what properties changed or why that could matter.

## Solution

The workbench turns amino-acid substitutions into readable analysis by comparing properties such as charge, polarity, hydropathy, volume, aromaticity, and substitution severity.

## Key Capabilities

- Amino acid property comparison
- BLOSUM62 and Grantham-style scoring concepts
- Hydropathy, polarity, charge, volume, and aromaticity analysis
- Guided evidence dossier generation
- Curated case library
- Teacher/student mode concepts
- Exportable educational materials
- Links/handoffs to UniProt, ClinVar, AlphaFold/PDB, and related resources

## Portfolio Value

This project is strong because it is useful, explainable, and grounded in an understandable educational problem.

## Responsible Scope

This should be framed as an educational and research-triage tool, not a clinical interpretation engine.
