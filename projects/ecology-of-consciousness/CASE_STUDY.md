# Ecology of Consciousness — Case Study

## Summary

Ecology of Consciousness is an interdisciplinary framework for analyzing digital environments as cognitive habitats. It focuses on how attention, meaning, self-regulation, agency, identity, and behavior are shaped by surrounding information environments.

## Core Constructs

- Attentional density
- Stimulus volatility
- Meaning coherence
- Digital habitat
- Cognitive residue

## Problem

Modern digital environments are often discussed in narrow terms: screen time, productivity, addiction, distraction, or mental health. This framework proposes a broader systems model for understanding how digital environments shape cognition and behavior.

## Solution

The framework treats a person’s digital environment as an ecology with measurable pressures and design properties. It includes practical tools such as a Cognitive Habitat Audit and Digital Environment Scorecard.

## Portfolio Value

This is the strongest original research/synthesis project. It shows conceptual originality, framework design, interdisciplinary thinking, and the ability to turn abstract ideas into practical evaluation tools.

## Responsible Scope

The project should not be marketed as a clinical mental-health tool. It is best framed as a cognitive, behavioral, media-ecology, and digital-environment framework.
